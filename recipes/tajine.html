Tajine

Tagine is a dish from the Maghreb countries of northwest Africa, prepared in a specific earthen vessel that bears the same name.
The recipe can be composed of meat; semolina and vegetables; and the characteristic taste and aroma is provided by the tabil.
This is a special mixture of tagine spices consisting of: coriander, cumin, garlic and paprika.

The tagines are cooked on hot coals or on wood. The container is made of clay and consists of two parts: the base, which is a deep
bowl; and the conical lid. The dishes are cooked in tagines for a very long time, which is why they get an original and unmistakable taste.

Chicken tajine ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, skin removed, cut into pieces
  • 2 large white or yellow onions, very finely chopped
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp saffron powder or 4 saffron threads
  • small turnips
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 2 tsp garlic, minced
  • 3 tsp cilantro, chopped
  • 3 tsp parsley, chopped
  • 1 handful green or red olives (or mixed)
  • 2 preserved lemons, quartered, seeds removed
  • ⅓ cup olive oil
  • 1 cup water, approximately
  • Salt, to taste


Instructions:

  1. Remove the flesh from the preserved lemons and finely chop it. And the
    chopped lemon flesh to a bowl with the chicken. Salt the chicken and mix well.
    If time allows, let the chicken marinate in the fridge for several hours or overnight.

  2. Distribute the onions in the tagine and arrange the chicken on top, followed by
    all the spices, preserved lemon and water. Cover, placing tagine on a diffuser if
    you’re using a flat stovetop, and set heat to medium-low.

  3. Give the tagine time to reach a simmer without uncovering. If you don’t hear
    the tagine simmering within 20 minutes, slightly increase the heat and then
    maintain the lowest heat setting required for a maintaining a gentle simmer.

  4. Turn the chicken over every 5 minutes. Cover the tagine and allow the chicken
    to finish cooking until very tender. Add the skin of preserved lemons, olive oil and green olives.

  5. Turn off the heat and let the tagine cool for about 10 to 15 minutes before
    serving. Moroccan tradition is to serve the food directly in the tagine, using
    Moroccan bread to scoop up the chicken and sauce. French fries are frequently
    served with this dish, and may even be placed on top of the chicken.
